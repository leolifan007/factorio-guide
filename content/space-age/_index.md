## Factorio Space Age

**Space Age** is Factorio's major expansion. It adds **5 unique planets**, each with distinct resources, production chains, and environmental hazards. Launching a rocket is still the goal, but now it sends you to new worlds.

---

## What's New in Space Age

### The 5 Planets

Each planet is a separate map connected by **interplanetary logistics**. You launch rockets to deliver items between planets.

| Planet | Environment | Key Resource | Challenge |
|--------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Nauvis** | Default | Standard ores | Biters |
| **Vulcanus** | Volcanic | Tungsten ore, calcite | Lava tiles, no biters |
| **Gleba** | Bioluminescent | Fruits, nutrients | Spoilage — organic products rot |
| **Fulgora** | Lightning | Scrap, holmium | Freezing, lightning power |
| **Aquilo** | Frozen | Cryogenic fluids, ice | Extremely cold, biters |

### New Mechanics

- **Quality Modules** — Craft items at higher quality tiers (normal → uncommon → rare → epic → legendary)
- **Quality Research** — Quality modules unlock with new science from each planet
- **Agricultural Science** — From Gleba's fruit production
- **Electromagnetic Science** — From Fulgora's holmium processing
- **Cryogenic Science** — From Aquilo's ice production
- **Heavy Modular Frame** — Vulcanus recipe for 3x the productivity
- **Interplanetary Logistics** — Rockets deliver items between planets

## Gleba — The Organic Planet

Gleba is the most mechanically complex planet. Organic products **spoil** if not processed quickly.

### The Spoilage Mechanic
- Fruits, nutrients, and biological products have a spoilage timer
- Spoiled products can be converted back to compost, but the cycle wastes time
- **Golden rule:** Process organic products **immediately** — no storage, no belts across long distances

### Production Chain
1. **Fruit** grows on Pentapod enemies when they die (spores)
2. **Fruit** → **Nutrients** (in bio-chamber)
3. **Nutrients** + **Rocket Fuel** → **Agricultural Science Packs**
4. Excess nutrients → **Compost** → back to **Fertilizer**

### Gleba Defense
- Pentapods spawn on Gleba and attack your base
- Biolab research can unlock bio-enchancement to tame pentapods
- **Tip:** Don't kill pentapods on the fruit processing loop — fruit spawns when they die

### Key Gleba Tips
- **Bio-chambers** replace assemblers for organic production
- **Bioflux** stabilizes spoilage (converts to storable product)
- Never ship raw organic products off-world — they spoil in transit

## Fulgora — The Lightning Planet

Fulgora runs on **lightning power** — a unique free energy source.

### The Lightning Mechanic
- Lightning strikes the highest point on Fulgora
- Lightning rods collect energy and store it in **capacitors**
- 1 lightning rod → ~800 kW average in the lightning zone
- Full lightning zone → ~50 MW from ~60 lightning rods

### Scrap Processing
- **Scrap** is the raw resource on Fulgora (free, unlimited, spawns from the sky)
- Scrap → **Components** (in Recycler)
- Components → **Holmium** (rare earth, used for quality science)
- Components → **Electronic Components** (used for Fulgora's unique electronics)

### Fulgora Defense
- No biters on Fulgora
- **Freezing** is the hazard — machines slow down in the cold zones
- Build in the warm equatorial belt for normal machine speeds

## Vulcanus — The Volcanic Planet

Vulcanus has **tungsten** — the strongest resource in the game.

### Key Resources
- **Tungsten ore** → **Tungsten carbide** (for heavy modular frames)
- **Calcite** → fixes calcium carbonate in recipes (replaces stone)
- **Lava** → used for Foundries (replaces smelting)

### Foundry vs. Furnace
The **Foundry** is Vulcanus' answer to electric furnaces:
- Smelts at 3x the speed of electric furnaces
- No power consumption (uses lava heat)
- Requires calcite and molten iron/copper input

### Vulcanus Defense
- **No biters** — no defense needed
- **Lava tiles** are impassable — plan your base layout around lava lakes
- Build using **Tungsten Carbide** heavy modular frames for extreme throughput

## Aquilo — The Frozen Planet

Aquilo is the final planet and the most challenging.

### Key Resources
- **Ice** → **Cryogenic fluids** → **Cryogenic Science Packs**
- No local ore — all ore must be imported from other planets
- **Low temperature coolant** — for nuclear plants on Aquilo

### The Cold Mechanic
- Everything on Aquilo requires heating to function
- Machines without a heat source go offline in the cold
- **Heat pipes** and **heat exchangers** are required infrastructure

### Aquilo Tips
- Bring **heavy modular frames** from Vulcanus to build your Aquilo base
- **Nuclear power** works on Aquilo with the right coolant setup
- Plan for self-sufficiency — Aquilo can't produce its own ores

## Quality Modules

Quality modules change the **tier** of items produced. Normal → Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary.

**Quality Modules** have 5 tiers, each increasing rarity:
- **Quality Module 1:** Uncommon (rare chance)
- **Quality Module 2:** Uncommon → Rare
- **Quality Module 3:** Rare → Epic
- **Quality Module 4:** Epic → Legendary
- **Quality Module 5:** Legendary guaranteed

**Use case:** A legendary heavy modular frame in a beaconed assembler produces 500 SPM with far fewer buildings.

## Interplanetary Logistics

Space Age adds **interplanetary logistics** — rockets that deliver items between planets.

### How It Works
1. Build an **Interplanetary Logistics System** (ILS) station
2. Set it as a **provider** (sends items to space) or **requester** (receives from space)
3. The system launches rockets automatically when conditions are met
4. **Orbital Collectors** in space collect items for return trips

### What to Ship Between Planets
- **Vulcanus → Nauvis:** Tungsten carbide, calcite
- **Fulgora → Nauvis:** Holmium, electronic components
- **Gleba → Nauvis:** Agricultural science (spoilage-safe when automated)
- **Nauvis → Aquilo:** Ore, modules (Aquilo can't produce ores)
