## Factorio Blueprints

Blueprints let you save any factory design and share it with other players. Factorio 1.1+ includes a built-in blueprint system, and the community has created an enormous library of tested, optimized blueprints for every stage of the game.

---

## What Are Blueprints?

A **blueprint** captures a section of your factory as a portable design. You can place it anywhere — in a new game, on a server, or share it with friends. Blueprints are stored as **strings** (a text code) that you can copy, paste, and share online.

The **Blueprint Book** groups multiple blueprints into a single library.

## Essential Blueprint Library

Every Factorio player should have (or build) these blueprints:

### 1. Smelting Block
- 24 electric furnaces on 2 parallel belts
- Iron and copper plates ready to bus
- Clean tile alignment for easy stacking
- **Why you need it:** Smelting is the foundation. A clean smelting blueprint makes rebuilding painless.

### 2. Main Bus Sub-Blocks
- Green circuit block (takes from bus, produces green circuits to bus)
- Red circuit block (requires green circuits + copper cable)
- Gear block (requires iron plate)
- Steel block (requires iron plate)

### 3. Science Pack Modules
- Red science block (fully automated, feeds from bus)
- Green science block
- Blue science block (the hardest — build this as a standalone unit first)
- Purple / Yellow science blocks

### 4. Mall Blueprint
A complete mall produces all the items you need without drawing from the main bus:
- Inserters (all types: fast, stack, bulk)
- Underground belts (all tiers)
- Splitters
- Assembling machines (1, 2, 3)
- Mining drills (electric, steam)
- Smelting columns
- Walls, turrets, ammo
- Rails, trains, train stops

### 5. Rail Intersections
- 4-way standard intersection (handles 4 trains simultaneously)
- 3-way T-junction
- Roundabout for 2-way traffic
- **Big 4-way** — for megabase rail networks with heavy traffic

### 6. Train Unloader
- Parallel unloading with inserters
- Balancer-based output to belts
- 4-wagon and 8-wagon versions

### 7. Nuclear Setup
- 2x2 reactor setup (480 MW)
- Heat pipe layout
- Turbine arrays
- Kovarex enrichment loop

### 8. Beaconed Production Blocks
- Beaconed green circuit block (8 speed 3 beacons, 1 assembler)
- Beaconed red circuit block
- Beaconed blue circuit block
- Beaconed rocket part block (with productivity 3 modules throughout)

## Where to Find Blueprints

### Built-In Blueprint Library (Factorio 1.1+)
Open the blueprint library (Ctrl+E), click "Import," and paste blueprint strings. Many community blueprints are shared as strings online.

### Factorio Blueprints Subreddit
[r/factorioblueprints](https://reddit.com/r/factorioblueprints) — the largest collection of community blueprints, organized by category.

### Factorio Blueprints Website
[factorioblueprints.com](https://factorioblueprints.com) — searchable database of player-submitted designs.

### Beltline and Other YouTube Creators
Many Factorio YouTubers release their personal blueprint books alongside tutorials.

## Creating Your Own Blueprints

### When to Blueprint
- When you've built something that works well
- Before you tear down an old section of your factory
- When you want to replicate a design in a new game

### Best Practices
1. **Plan first, blueprint second** — don't blueprint spaghetti
2. **Include space around the blueprint** — leave 1 tile of clearance for underground belt exits
3. **Document the design** — add a comment with the name, purpose, and any special notes
4. **Use tileable designs** — make sure blueprints can be placed side-by-side without gaps

### Blueprint Comments
Right-click a building in blueprint mode to add a comment. Comments are useful for:
- Naming the design ("4-beacon green circuit")
- Explaining resource requirements ("Requires 1 belt of iron + 1 belt of copper")
- Listing mod dependencies

## Blueprint String Sharing

A blueprint string is a base64-encoded representation of your blueprint. To share:
1. Open the blueprint library
2. Click the blueprint
3. Click "Export"
4. Copy the string
5. Share it (on Reddit, Discord, etc.)

To import someone else's blueprint:
1. Open your blueprint library
2. Click "Import from string"
3. Paste the string
4. The blueprint appears in your library
