## TL;DR

Factorio is a game about building factories. Your first goal: automate red science packs. Everything before that is a tutorial disguised as the game. Here's how to not waste your first 10 hours.

---

## The First Five Minutes

You crash on a planet. That's it. That's the premise.

Don't overthink your starting area. Pick a direction and start walking. Collect wood by hand, hand-craft your first assembler, and set up a basic furnace line. I spent my first run terrified of leaving my starting area — that was a mistake. The map is huge, resources are everywhere, and you can always come back.

Here's what you need to know right now: **everything in Factorio is solved by more inserters and more belts.** Not better design. Not optimization. More of the basics, faster.

## Setting Up Your First Ore Outposts

Your starting patch won't last. It's fine for early red science, but green circuits and steel production will eat through it fast.

Move to a new ore patch when your current one drops below 1M ore remaining. Look for ore patches of at least 1M on your map (default hotkey: `M`). Click the ore to see its size before committing to an outpost.

For your first outpost:

1. Drop a requester chest connected to your main bus via logistic bots, or run a dedicated belt
2. Set up smelting right at the ore patch — bring plates back, not ore
3. Use steel furnaces, not iron. Trust me on this.

## The Red Science Problem

Red science is the gate to everything. To automate it, you need:

- **Inserters** — move items between machines and belts
- **Assemblers** — craft components
- **Transport belts** — move items from place to place

The most common beginner mistake: putting everything too close together. Your first factory will outgrow its space in about 2 hours. Build wider than you think you need. Leave room for parallelism.

## Smelting: Where New Players Struggle

Smelting is dead simple once you understand the ratio.

Basic ratio: **1 stone furnace or 1 steel furnace can supply exactly 1 assembler.**

Steel furnaces are twice as fast as stone furnaces and don't need fuel (they're heated by the coal you insert). Use steel furnaces for everything past yellow belt.

The smelter setup I use at the start:

```
[Coal] → [Steel Furnace] → [Iron Plates] → [Belt]
              ↓
         [Inserter]
```

Keep it simple. Extend as needed.

## Your First Bus

A bus is a set of parallel belts down the center of your factory, each carrying one resource. Everything downstream pulls from the bus.

Common bus resources (in order):
1. Iron plates
2. Copper plates
3. Steel plates
4. Green circuits
5. Gears
6. Lubricant / plastic / circuits as needed

Don't over-engineer your first bus. Four belts each of iron and copper is enough for red + green science. Add more lanes as you need them.

## The Toolbelt Is Your Best Friend

You can hold 5 blueprints, 5 stack inserters, and a bunch of other things in your toolbelt. But the thing that changed my game: **the deconstruction planner.**

`Shift + Click` a building with the deconstruction planner selected to mark it for removal. Robots will come, pick it up, and return it to your inventory. This is how you expand without rebuilding by hand.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

**1. Building too compact**
Give yourself room. You will expand. Your factory always gets bigger than you planned.

**2. Not using stack inserters early enough**
Regular inserters are fine for a few machines. Stack inserters (yellow) move a full stack at once. Use them for anything that's moving a lot of items.

**3. Ignoring pollution**
Biters will show up. They don't care about your factory. Get military science going early, set up turrets, and automate bullet production before biters become a crisis.

**4. Trying to use circuits on day one**
Circuits are powerful but complex. Get your basic factory running first. Then come back with blueprint books.

**5. Hand-crafting too long**
The moment you find yourself crafting things by hand repeatedly, that's a signal: automate it. If you're hand-crafting belts, inserters, or circuits for more than 5 minutes straight, stop and build an assembler.

## What Actually Matters

At the end of your first session, you want to have:

- A smelting array (at least 24 steel furnaces)
- Red science automated (at least 1 assembler, ideally 2)
- Green science automated (requires circuits — one assembler is enough to start)
- A defensive perimeter (at least a few turrets and some ammo production)

Everything else is details. Don't optimize. Don't blueprint. Just get it running.

---

## Bottom Line

Factorio rewards getting things done more than getting things perfect. Your first factory will be ugly, cramped, and inefficient. That's fine. The goal is to have a working base that can research rocket parts. Everything else is refinement.

Play the game. Automate everything. Expand outward. That's the loop.
