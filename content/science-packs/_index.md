## Science Packs in Factorio

Science packs are the currency of research. Each pack represents a "unit" of research, and manufacturing them efficiently is the core challenge of Factorio's mid and late game.

There are **7 tiers** of science packs:

| Tier | Name | Color | Key Ingredients |
|------|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | Red Science Pack | Red | 1 Copper Cable + 1 Iron Gear |
| 2 | Green Science Pack | Green | 1 Electronic Circuit |
| 3 | Military Science Pack | Grey | 1 Grenade + 1 Submachine Gun |
| 4 | Blue Science Pack | Blue | 1 Advanced Circuit + 1 Engine Unit |
| 5 | Purple Science Pack | Purple | 1 Electric Furnace + 1 Flying Robot Frame |
| 6 | Yellow Science Pack | Yellow | 1 Bulk Inserter + 1 Beacon |
| 7 | Space Science Pack | White | 1 Rocket Part + 1 Satellite |

---

## Red Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Copper Cable + 1 Iron Gear Wheel → 1 Red Science Pack

Red Science is your first automated research. No intermediates needed — just copper and iron.

**Ratio:** 1 assembler making cables, 1 making gears, 1 making red packs. A simple balanced setup.

---

## Green Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Electronic Circuit → 1 Green Science Pack

Electronic circuits (green circuits) are the bottleneck. Each green circuit requires:
- 1 Copper Plate
- 3 Iron Plates (made into 1.5 cables — you'll need extra copper cable production)

**Ratio:** For every 3 green circuit assemblers, you'll want about 2 copper cable assemblers.

---

## Military Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Grenade + 1 Submachine Gun Magazine → 1 Military Science Pack

Grenades require coal and copper plates. Submachine guns require iron plates.

**Tip:** Produce grenades and ammo on the same belt. Both are consumed at the same rate.

---

## Blue Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Advanced Circuit + 1 Engine Unit → 1 Blue Science Pack

Advanced circuits (red circuits) require:
- 2 Electronic Circuits
- 2 Copper Cable
- 0.1 Advanced Circuit (via production bonus)

Engine units require:
- 1 Steel Plate
- 1 Iron Plate
- 2 Pipe

**This is the most complex science pack.** Build it in stages: plastic → red circuits → engine units → blue science.

---

## Purple Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Electric Furnace + 1 Flying Robot Frame → 1 Purple Science Pack

Flying robot frames require:
- 1 Battery
- 1 Electronic Circuit
- 1 Iron Gear Wheel

Electric furnaces require steel and circuit inputs.

---

## Yellow Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Bulk Inserter + 1 Beacon → 1 Yellow Science Pack

Both the bulk inserter and beacon require productivity modules for efficient research speed.

**Late game tip:** Productivity modules in assemblers reduce the total number of packs you need to produce.

---

## Space Science Pack

**Recipe:** 1 Rocket Part + 1 Satellite → 1 Space Science Pack

The final science — and the most satisfying. Building a rocket silo and launching the satellite is the primary win condition.

**Rocket parts require:** Low Density Structure + Rocket Control Unit + Rocket Fuel

**The satellite** requires: Kovarex enrichment (or manual uranium processing) for nuclear fuel cells.

---

## Science Progression Tips

1. **Never pause lower-tier science** — if green science stops, blue science stops. Keep lower tiers fed.
2. **Build in ratios** — match production to your research speed. Excess science packs back up in containers.
3. **Use direct insertion** — inserter → assembler → inserter is cleaner than belts for most intermediate products.
4. **Plan for expansion** — leave room to double production when you upgrade to modules and beacons.
5. **Blue science is the real test** — if blue science runs smoothly, your factory architecture is solid.
